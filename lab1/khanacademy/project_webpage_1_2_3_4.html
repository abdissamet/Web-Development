Our first animal is Exotic Birds.

Exotic parrots are colorful and smart birds from tropical regions. They can mimic sounds and human speech. Popular types include Macaws, Cockatoos, African Grey Parrots, and Amazon Parrots. They live in forests and eat fruits, nuts, and seeds. Exotic parrots are social and need attention. They can live for many years and make popular, but demanding, pets.

Our second animal is Tiger.

Exotic tigers are rare and beautiful big cats found mostly in Asia. They have striking orange fur with black stripes, which helps them camouflage in forests and grasslands. Tigers are powerful predators that hunt deer, wild boar, and other animals. Some exotic types include Bengal, Siberian, Sumatran, and Malayan tigers. They are endangered due to habitat loss and poaching, so conservation efforts are very important.

Our third animal is Exotic Monkey.

Exotic monkeys are small to medium-sized primates found in tropical forests of Asia, Africa, and Central and South America. They are smart, social, and often live in groups. They eat fruits, leaves, insects, and sometimes small animals. Popular exotic monkeys include Capuchins, Howler monkeys, and Spider monkeys. Many species are endangered because of deforestation and illegal pet trade.

Our last destination is the waterfall.

An exotic waterfall is a beautiful and unusual natural water feature, often found in tropical or remote areas. Water flows over rocks or cliffs, creating stunning sights and sounds. Exotic waterfalls are surrounded by lush vegetation, attracting tourists and nature lovers.
